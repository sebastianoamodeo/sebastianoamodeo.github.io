ANALYSING THE DATA¶

I am going to use two Visualization Functions (line-plot and scatter-plot)¶

In [5]:
plt.style.use("fivethirtyeight")
plt.rcParams["figure.figsize"] = (12,8)

def plot_mean_duration_in_last_years(movies, label, color):
    recent_movies = movies[(movies["release_year"]>=2011) & (movies["release_year"]<=2020)]
    mean_duration_by_year = recent_movies.groupby("release_year")["duration"].apply(np.median).apply(int)
    plt.title("Mean movies duration")
    plt.ylabel("mean duration (min)")
    plt.xlabel("release year")
    plt.ylim([80, 120])
    plt.plot(mean_duration_by_year, label=label, c=color)
    plt.legend()

def scatter_movies_durations(movies, label, color):
    plt.title("Duration of each Movie")
    plt.xlabel("release year")
    plt.ylabel("duration (min)")
    plt.scatter(movies["release_year"], movies["duration"], 
                label=label, c=color, alpha=0.3)
    plt.legend()

CONCLUSIONS¶

Even if there is a general decrease in movies duration, with a closer look we can see a few category are mostly responsible for that.

In particular Documentaries, Children Shows and Stand-Up Comedy are the most time decreasing categories.

As the last plot shows, removing those categories increases dramatically the slope of our average duration curve.
